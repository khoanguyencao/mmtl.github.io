Research Projects

We are currently working on the following projects/questions:

1) Medical AI Evaluation
Current medical AI evaluation datasets (e.g. MMLU, MedQA, MedMCQA) represent rudimentary measures of clinical knowledge. This project aims to compile several hundred thousand permissive medical case studies and synthetically generate over a million case studies for more robust medical AI evaluation.

2) Retrieval Augmented Systems in Clinical Practice
Clinicians face an overwhelming proliferation of clinical guidelines (e.g. nearly 400 in OBGYN). In collaboration with Monash Health, Victoria's largest health service, this project aims to harness the power of RAG systems to rapidly answer doctors' questions to provide concise, relevant and up-to-date clinical guidance.

3) Immunology Foundation Models
Foundation models represent an incredibly powerful tool to model complex biological interactions. In collaboration with the GIN Discovery Program, this project will build a multi-omics gut immunology foundation model to generate novel hypothesis for the research field.

4) Clinical Agents
AI agents and text to action represent a powerful new clinical paradigm. This project aims to identify the most effective agentic workflows in medicine, including key research questions in intra-agent communication and observability/trust.

5) Regulation and Fairness of Medical AI
Significant questions remain on how exactly to regulate medical AI and ensure its fairness. In collaboration with Monash Bioethics, this project aims to provide policy recommendations on how AI should be regulated and assess fairness alignment strategies.
